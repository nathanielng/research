Career Profile

My career interests are computational algorithms, machine learning and statistics as applied to the AME (advanced manufacturing & manufacturing) domain. My work is focused on materials and analytics; it involves scientific code development incorporating physics-based models targeting supercomputer clusters.

Material models have been developed for alloys and ferroelectrics at the microstructure scale, and incorporate thermodynamics & elasticity considerations.

Keywords: high-performance computing • optimization • partial differential equations • iterative solvers • computer vision

Research

Current Projects
 

Machine Learning in Materials Science - currently developing materials databases and machine learning pipelines for materials science.

Structure Optimization with Genetic Algorithms and Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials - developing machine-learning interatomic potentials (MLIP) for use in LAMMPS and coupled with structure optimization calculations using genetic algorithms.
